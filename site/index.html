World Cup 2026 — Chemistry

Player chemistry between every pair on every WC 2026 squad, computed from their actual international matches.

How this works

The metric

JOI90 — Joint Offensive Impact per 90 minutes. For each consecutive same-team action pair (e.g., a pass from A → control by B), we sum the VAEP value of both actions. We aggregate per pair across every match where the two played together, then normalize to a per-90-minutes-shared rate.

The data

Event data from StatsBomb Open Data: every on-ball action in WC 2022 (more international tournaments rolling in over time). Each nation's roster is auto-derived from the top-26 most-capped players in that window.

The methodology

Based on Bransen & Van Haaren, Player Chemistry: Striving for a Perfectly Balanced Soccer Team (MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference 2020). Their original work used Wyscout club data; this project flips the methodology to international matches.

Caveats

Action values come from a heuristic VAEP estimator in v1 — defenders and goalkeepers may rank higher than they should because long progressive passes get over-rewarded. A trained VAEP model is queued for v2. Seven nations (AUT, CIV, COL, EGY, ITA, NGA, PAR) have no chemistry data yet — they did not appear in WC 2022 events.
