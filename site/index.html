World Cup 2026 — Chemistry

Offensive chemistry between every pair on every WC 2026 squad — measured three different ways, scored across four men's international tournaments. Toggle metrics below; click any nation for its pitch chart.

How this works

The metric — three views

Toggle between xT (Karun Singh's Expected Threat — rewards moving the ball into more dangerous pitch zones), VAEP (a trained classifier — rewards actions that raise the probability of scoring in the next few seconds), and xG-Chain (credits a pair with the xG of every possession both touched). All three are normalized per 90 shared minutes. G and A columns show the sum of the pair's goals and assists across the window.

The data

Event data from StatsBomb Open Data — every on-ball action across FIFA WC 2022, UEFA Euro 2024, UEFA Euro 2020, and Copa América 2024: 262 matches, ~427K SPADL actions. Curated WC 2026 squads (May 2026 call-ups and announced final rosters) where available; auto-derived from each nation's top-capped players in the window otherwise.

The methodology

Based on Bransen & Van Haaren, Player Chemistry: Striving for a Perfectly Balanced Soccer Team (MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference 2020). The paper computes JOI over Wyscout club matches; this project flips it to international matches and adds the xT and xG-Chain views alongside VAEP. Pipeline built on socceraction.

Caveats

xT over-credits patient backline buildup — two CBs cycling possession still accumulate xT. VAEP is trained on only ~427K actions (~4.3K goal events) so the absolute scale is small; rankings are reliable, individual values less so. xG-Chain's underlying xG model is a lightweight logistic regression on ~5K shots — directionally correct, conservative on absolutes. A few qualified nations (e.g., Curaçao, Haiti, New Zealand, Cape Verde) have little StatsBomb coverage and will show sparse chemistry data.
