World Cup 2026 — Chemistry

Player chemistry between every pair on every WC 2026 squad, scored by xT (Expected Threat) across five international tournaments.

How this works

The metric

JOI-xT — Joint Expected Threat per 90 minutes. For each consecutive same-team action pair, we sum the xT (Expected Threat) delta of both actions. xT values each pitch location by the probability a team scores from that location within the next few actions — actions that move the ball into more dangerous zones are worth more. We aggregate per pair across every match where the two played together, then normalize to a per-90-minutes-shared rate.

The data

Event data from StatsBomb Open Data: every on-ball action in WC 2022, Euro 2024, Copa América 2024, Euro 2020, and the FIFA Women's World Cup 2023. Each nation's roster is auto-derived from the top-26 most-capped players in that window.

The methodology

Based on Bransen & Van Haaren, Player Chemistry: Striving for a Perfectly Balanced Soccer Team (MIT Sloan Sports Analytics Conference 2020). Their original work used Wyscout club data; this project flips the methodology to international matches.

Caveats

We currently use xT (Karun Singh, 2018) for action values. xT is a positional grid model — it doesn't reward dribbles, take-ons, or pressure actions as much as a fully-trained VAEP model would. A trained-VAEP view is queued for v2. Some nations (e.g., Italy, Egypt, Nigeria) didn't qualify for any of our covered tournaments so have no chemistry data.
